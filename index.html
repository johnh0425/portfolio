About Me

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."
- Steve Jobs

This is the story of how my journey led me to a deep passion for the world of technology.

The Root of my Passion.

As a child, I was always building—first with LEGO, then in my dad's woodshop. But it was at nine years old, when he enrolled me in a 3D design class using 3Ds Max, that my fascination with computers truly began. I became captivated by the intersection of technology, design, and engineering—how digital tools could bring ideas to life. That early spark continues to fuel my passion today.

My Crucial Decision.

I started my college journey at the UCR, studying Mechanical Engineering. I had always loved building and thought it was the perfect path for me. But as I explored different fields, I realized that Computer Engineering was the bridge between my passion for development and my fascination with computers. After taking my first coding class and later diving into computer architecture, I knew I had found my place and made the crucial decision to switch majors. Two years in, I transferred to UCSD , where my ambitions only grew, driven by a desire to push the boundaries of technology and innovation.

What More to do than Practice.

My journey as a computer engineer truly began in December 2023 with my first group coding project—a Chess game built in C++. I quickly grew to love the planning and development process, seeing how ideas transformed into functional software. From there, I expanded my skills into web development and, more recently, into building Machine Learning models. Lately, I've been diving into hardware design, developing projects in embedded systems with the goal of one day exploring the world of computer chip design. With every project, I refine my craft, always eager to push my technical abilities further.

Free Time

When I'm not working, you'll probably find me gaming with friends, shooting hoops on the basketball court, or unwinding with my dad while watching sports. Whether it's the strategy of a game, the competition on the court, or just kicking back with good company, I always find ways to stay engaged and recharge.
